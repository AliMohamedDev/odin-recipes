Ethiopian Spiced Chicken Stew (Doro Wat)

Doro Wat, also known as Doro Wot, is a traditional Ethiopian stew that features tender chicken simmered in a rich, spicy berbere sauce. This flavorful dish is renowned for its deep, robust flavors, created by slow-cooking the chicken with onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of aromatic spices. Often served with injera, a type of Ethiopian flatbread, Doro Wat is a hearty and satisfying meal that brings the unique tastes of Ethiopian cuisine to your table.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken drumsticks and thighs, skin removed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons berbere spice blend
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Steps

  1. Prepare the chicken
  2. Cook the onions
  3. Add garlic and ginge
  4. Add the spices
  5. Add tomato paste and broth
  6. Simmer the chicken
  7. Add the eggs
  8. Serve
